Design Guide

Determine the best design for your Experiment

The Design Guide is a step-through guide to help you decide which experimental design and statistical method is most appropriate for your study. Enter in your study information and the Design Guide will suggest an Experimental Design and Statistical Test that is most suited to the details entered as well as any alternatives that may possible.

Embedded in the Design Guide is also a section on calculating Statistical Power and Sample Size based on your inputs. If the Statistical Power and/or Sample Size calculated are not feasible or sufficient for your study, you may need to modify the information to meet your experimental needs.

On this page, enter in the details about the Treatments and Controls that will be tested.

For Treatments, click the + Add Treatment box to add the appropriate number of Treatments that will be used. For each Treatment, enter in the total number of settings within that treatment. Continue to next page by clicking Next Section.

Our Case Study has one Treatment (the effect of the Drug) and no additional Control. This Treatment has four levels (One control and three settings of the drug used).

On this page, enter in any Nontreatment Factors (currently only Categorical Factors can be chosen) that will be used which are beyond the treatments selected on the previous section and may influence the outcome measure of interest. These are variables that are used to group subjects by similarities to help reduce bias in the analysis of results.

Be sure to click Check Your Design at the bottom of the page before continuing to the next page.

Our Case Study has zero Categorical Factors and zero Covariates, and so no information is added in on this page.


Clicking Check Your Design will bring up a summary page which list all groups suggested by the treatment and control design. Continue to next page by clicking Yes! Next Section.

This page presents the recommended statistical test to analyze the results based on inputs in previous pages. The results, either sample size or statistical power of your choice, are displayed. You must simulate your design before moving on to generate the summary report.

The simulation page lets you simulate random experiments to get a sense of the outcome you expect from your experiment. Continue to next page by clicking Next Section.

Our Case Study recommends 1-Way ANOVA and 12 samples size per group to achieve 0.05 significance level and 0.8 statistical power.


Simulation page.
Each time you click the Simulate Random Experiment button, a random experiment is simulated and a different p-value will result. You can enter your desired "Significance Level" and run the simulation. Continue to next page by clicking Generate Report.

On this page, the Design Guide will analyze the information that you entered and suggest a Design Type and a Statistical Test that are most appropriate for your study. If applicable, Alternative Design Types will be suggested (although these will likely require you to think about redefining your study).

The report will also include the power and sample size information calculated on the previous page, as well as three randomly-generated simulations to display in your report.

Our Case Study is a Completely Randomized Design and should be analyzed using a 1-Way ANOVA.
