Case Study 3: Repeated Measures Design
Effects of NAAG peptidase inhibitor on spatial learning and memory after traumatic brain injury in rats.

objective

Traumatic brain injury (TBI) leads to a rapid and excessive elevation in extracellular glutamate in brain which is strongly associated with excitotoxicity and behavioral deficits. The peptide neurotransmitter, N-acetylaspartylglutamate (NAAG), is co-released with glutamate during periods of excessive brain excitation. NAAG activates pre-synaptic autoreceptors that suppress neurotransmitter release. However, extracellular NAAG is rapidly hydrolyzed in the synapse by peptidase activity. In theory, inhibiting NAAG peptidase activity would increase the concentration of NAAG in the synapse and thereby inhibit further glutamate release and reduce excitotoxicity.

  • In this study, the NAAG peptidase inhibitor, PGI-02776 was tested for effects on behavioral performance in the Morris water maze (MWM) after experimental TBI in rats.

hypothesis

Pharmacological inhibition of NAAG peptidase with PGI-02776 will reduce cognitive deficits associated with experimental TBI.

methods

Subjects were assigned to 3 groups and performance was recorded over 5 consecutive days;

Day {{::day+1}}
Sham TBI control TBI + saline vehicle TBI + 10mg/kg PGI-02776 Subject {{::subj+1}} {{::num+1}}

Anesthetized rats were administered a moderate magnitude of TBI. Thirty minutes after TBI, PGI-02776 or saline vehicle was administered intraperitoneal. A sham control group was subjected to identical surgical and anesthetic procedures with the exception of receiving a TBI. Rats were tested in the MWM on days 11-15 after TBI.

Rats received a total of 4 MWM trials per day from different starting point, over 5 consecutive days. Mean latency to find the submerged, hidden platform was calculated for each day to assess learning.

statistics

A 3(group) X 5(assessment days) repeated measures ANOVA with assessment days as the repeated variable within subjects followed by Dunnett’s test comparing each TBI group to sham control.

results

There was a significant main effect of time [F(4,88)=96.92, p < 0.001] indicating that all groups learned the task over time. There was a significant time by group interaction [F(8,88)=4.06, p < 0.001] indicating that the rate of learning differed between groups. There was a significant main effect of group on latency to find the platform [F(2,22)=8.719, p < 0.005]. Post hoc Dunnett’s test indicated that over 5 days the TBI + saline group took significantly more time to locate the hidden platform (p < 0.001) compared to the sham TBI group on days 11-15 post-injury. Latency to locate the hidden platform was not significantly different between the TBI+PGI-02776 group and sham TBI group. Variation bars in the figure are standard error of the mean.

conclusions

TBI produced significant cognitive deficits compared to sham. The performance of TBI animals treated with the NAAG peptidase inhibitor, PGI-02776 was not significantly different from the sham control group. Thus, PGI-02776 improved cognitive performance measured by spatial learning and memory in the MWM.
