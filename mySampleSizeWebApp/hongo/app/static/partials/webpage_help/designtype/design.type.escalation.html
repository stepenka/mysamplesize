Dose Escalation

Dose Escalation designs investigate the effect of a drug on a measured response by giving each subject an increasing dose level of a drug over time.

This design is similar to a dose response design but this design has repeated measures. In this design, all doses of a compound are given to each subject over time in an increasing non-random order.

The use of a continuous variable may more closely reflect the biology of an experiment making it a more appropriate design for your experimental goals than a factorial design. For example, if you are more interested in if the results show an statistically significant effect of a dose at level 3 compared to level 4 and 5 instead of if the effect of level 3 is statistically significantly different from the control. This allows a researcher to better assess the dose-response relationship instead of the effect of each dose level compared to the control.

example

A researcher is interested in testing the effect of a drug on a subject. 16 subjects are divided into 4 equal cohorts. The drug is divided into 3 dosage levels and a placebo for 4 treatment levels. The latency of a physical response is measured from each subject.

Table of experimental design for one cohort (Whitehead et al., 2001);

Experimental Period Subject 1 Subject 2 Subject 3 Subject 4
1 placebo d1 d1 d1
2 d1 placebo d2 d2
3 d2 d2 placebo d2
4 d3 d3 d3 placebo

statistical tests

Statistical tests that are commonly appropriate for this design:

  • Linear Regression
  • Generalized linear model
