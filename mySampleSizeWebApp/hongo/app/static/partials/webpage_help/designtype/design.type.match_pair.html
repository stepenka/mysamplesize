Matched Pair

A Matched Pair design is a variation of a Randomized Block design where 2 treatments are applied to subjects grouped into pairs. Each treatment type is applied randomly to one subject within the pair. Pairs are usually grouped based on a common covariate.

example

A researcher is interested in testing the effectiveness of a treatment (T) compared to a control (C). 10 individuals volunteer to participate in this study. Individuals are divided into pairs depending on gender and age group;

Subject 1 Subject 2
Pair 1 T C
Pair 2 C T
Pair 3 C T
Pair 4 T C
Pair 5 C T

statistical tests

Statistical tests that are commonly appropriate for this design:

  • ANOVA
  • Regression
