Treatments factors define the ways in which you make designed changes your experimental test subjects to examine your scientific hypothesis. An experiment may have one or more treatments, and each treatment may have multiple levels (dosages, for example). In most experiments, different treatments will require their own controls.

It is important to distinguish controlled treatment factors that the experimenter will set by design and apply to subjects from nontreatment factors (sometimes called confounders or fixed factors) like sex, age, or weight, that the experimenters DO NOT choose. These nontreatment factors that may impact the outcome measure will be entered on the next tab, Nontreatment Factors.
