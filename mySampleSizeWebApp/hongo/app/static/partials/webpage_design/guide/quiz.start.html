When designing an experiment it is important to have a clearly-defined scientific premise to be investigated. You will need to identify an outcome measure, and the units of the measure, that you will use to study your hypothesis. These ideas form the foundation of the statistical analyses that will help answer your scientific questions.

Warning:
To use your outcome measure to study your hypothesis, you will need to have some idea of the variability in your outcome in terms of your subjects (i.e., the standard deviation). Typically this standard deviation is estimated through a pilot study, by examining similar experiments in the published literature, or by previous related experiments in your own lab. You will also need to have an idea of how much your treatments, manipulations, or experimental factors impact the outcome measure (i.e., an ). As with the standard deviation, knowledge about relevant effect size values usually comes from pilot studies or prior published work.
