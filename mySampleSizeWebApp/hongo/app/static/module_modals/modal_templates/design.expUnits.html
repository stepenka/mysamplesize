Experimental Unit

The experimental unit is the smallest division of experimental material such that any two experimental units can receive different treatments.


Typically, an experimental unit is an individual subject. However, other experimental units may be:

  • Cage of subjects
    (applying a treatment to a group)
  • Area of skin on a subject
    (applying treatments to different patches of skin)


Failure to properly identify the experimental unit can lead to inflated Type-I error rates in hypothesis testing.

Blair, R. C., Higgins, J. J., Topping, M. E. H., and Mortimer, A. L. (1983), “An Investigation of the Robustness of the t Test to Unit of Analysis Violations,” Educational and Psychological Measurement, 43: 69.
