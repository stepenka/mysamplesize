Case Study 4: Repeated Measures Design
A 3 Way ANOVA (gene X treatment) design with repeated measures (2 X 2 X 6)

objective

To test our hypothesis we will examine the role of serotonin 2A receptor activation in the locomotor effect of the hallucinogen DOI by comparing the effects of DOI in wild-type (WT) mice with DOI effects in mice that do not carry the serotonin 2A receptor gene (gene knock-out, KO). The design involves using mice with a genetic mutation to selectively dissect out the serotonin2A component of DOI effects. (Halberstadt et al. 2009)

  • This study examined the effects of serotonin 2A receptor activation in the locomotor effect of the hallucinogen DOI.

hypothesis

Serotonin 2A receptor activation is a central mechanism for the behavioral effects of serotonergic hallucinogens.

methods

Subjects were randomly assigned to one of the following 4 groups and distance traveled was measured every 10 minutes over a 60 minute time period.

WT KO vehicle DOI 1 mg/kg subject {{::subj+1}} {{::num+1}}
  • serotonin 2A receptor gene (gene, 2 levels: WT or KO)
  • DOI (treatment, 2 levels: vehicle vs. DOI 1 mg/kg)

Mice were treated IP with either vehicle or DOI and placed in the locomotor chamber for 60 min.

Activity over the 60 min period will be binned into 10 min blocks to examine changes in drug response over time.

The outcome measured was the distance travelled (cm) by the animal over a 60 min time period after drug treatment in a standard locomotor chamber.

statistics

A 3 way repeated measures ANOVA (gene and drug as between subject factors and time as a within subject factor) is conducted followed by Tukey’s test comparing drug to vehicle in each genotype.

results

A 3 way interaction between time, gene and drug was observed (F(5,215)=2.45, p<0.04). Tukey’s post-hoc comparisons at each time point indicate that DOI significantly (p<0.05) increased locomotor activity in WT mice at the 30-60 min time points, while KO mice showed no effects of DOI treatment on activity at any time.

Halberstadt, A. L., van der Heijden, I., Ruderman, M. A., Risbrough, V. B., Gingrich, J. A., Geyer, M. A., & Powell, S. B. (2009). 5-HT2A and 5-HT2C receptors exert opposing effects on locomotor activity in mice. Neuropsychopharmacology, 34(8), 1958–1967.
