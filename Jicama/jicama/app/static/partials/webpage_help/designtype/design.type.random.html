Completely Randomized

A Completely Randomized Design involves a single factor, usually the treatment, that is assigned to the experimental unit completely randomly. Experimental subjects are assigned at random (often assigned by a computer generated method). Can have uneven numbers in each group.

example

A researcher is interested in testing the effect on a response between 2 drugs so that there are 3 levels to this treatment (Control, Drug A, and Drug B). 18 individuals volunteer to participate in this study. Using a random number generator, each individual is assigned to one of the three treatments;

Control Drug A Drug B
2 15 18
17 16 6
5 12 3
8 1 10
13 11 9
7 4 14

statistical tests

Statistical tests that are commonly appropriate for this design:

  • One-Way ANOVA
  • Linear Regression
