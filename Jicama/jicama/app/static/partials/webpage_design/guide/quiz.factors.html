Next, we consider nontreatment quantities -- beyond the treatments selected on the previous page -- that may influence the outcome measure of interest. These may be categorical factors such as sex or litter, or continuous covariates such as body mass. Often these quantities are referred to as confounding variables, since the effect of the treatment on the subjects may be moderated by them.

It is important to distinguish experimenter-designed treatment factors from these nontreatment factors, so we ask you to enter them here on a separate tab rather than as treatments factors on the previous tab.

Categorical factors are discrete variables.



A covariate is a continuous variable corresponding to a characteristic of the subject.
